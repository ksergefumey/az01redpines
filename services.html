DIGITAL SCHOOLS & COMPUTER LABS

03. The DigiSchools Program.

Activities & Services.

Within the framework of this program, we have adopted three general approaches to the instructional use of computers and the Internet:

  • Learning about computers: in which technological literacy is the end goal.
  • Learning with computers: in which technology facilitates learning across the curriculum..
  • Learning though computers: integrating technological skills development with curriculum applications.

The activities and services offered by the project are centered around these three approaches. Those activities are:

  • Set up of Laboratory Facilities and Learning Hubs
  • Curriculum Development
  • Teachers training on use of ICT in an educational context
  • Students training on use of ICT
  • Creation and development of an educational platform utilizing a wiki service
  • Technical Assistance
  • Technical Training
  • Creation of a Computer Laboratory Maintenance Protocol
Activity
Set up of Laboratory Facilities and Learning Hubs

Purpose

  • Provide computer devices to students and teachers, printers, projectors, as well as an IT system and network infrastructure to schools.
  • Provide libraries of digital educational content.

Target

  • +200 students / year (20 students / school / year).
  • 20 teachers / year (2 teachers / school / year).

Content

  • 10 Computer Laboratory Facilities and Learning Hubs will be set up.
  • Every facility will be equipped with 20 computer devices using a locally stored digital educational content, as well as video projector and printers. We will install Internet-ready equipment for mobile broadband.
  • For the educational content, we will use RACHEL which is a portable plug-and-play server which stores educational websites and makes that content available over any local (offline) wireless connection. Once connected to RACHEL, schools can instantly access offline versions of the world's best free educational websites including Wikipedia, Khan Academy, and over 100 more.
  • An IT network infrastructure allowing to link all the computer labs to a server room from where we can monitor and detect all technical incidents to maintain the equipment in operational condition.

Output

  • 10 computer Laboratory Facilities and Learning Hubs set up.
  • 10 Libraries of educational content set up in 10 schools.
  • +200 computer devices made available for students and teachers.

Activity
Curriculum Development

Purpose

To connect curriculum to the learning goals and project objectives associated with ICT4E.

Target

  • +200 students / year (20 students / school / year).
  • 20 teachers / year (2 teachers / school / year).

Content

  • Identify ICT resources that can serve the general purpose and specific elements of teaching and learning in subjects, cross-curricular areas, and integrated studies.
  • Encourage school-based efforts to adapt ICT to different learning areas, subjects, and interests.
  • Encourage schools to use ICT to make internal assessment less labor intensive.

Output

Inclusion of ICT into the curriculum at the different levels as a competency.

Activity
Teacher training on use of ICT in an educational context

Purpose

To train teachers ensuring that they have a solid foundation on the use of the ICT.

Target

20 teachers / year (2 teachers / school / year).

Content

This training activity will be implemented around 3 modules:
Module 1 - Hardware:
This is the basic level of skills required for the use of computers in any application. These mechanical skills must be acquired before any unsupervised usage of a computer is possible.
Module 2 - Software:
Building upon the basic mechanical skills, it becomes possible to learn the use of widely used computer applications.
Module 3 - Teaching and Learning Administration:
The learner starts to apply application software to the management and process of education.

Output

Inclusion of ICT into the curriculum at the different levels as a competency.

Activity
Students training on use of ICT

Purpose

To train participants on the core, intermediate and advanced technical training to students on Linux OS, Windows OS, and Cloud Technologies (MS Azure, AWS and GCP), ensuring that these students have a solid foundation of skills related to these OS and Cloud Platforms.

Target

+ 200 students / year (20 students / school / year).

Output

200 students trained by year.

Activity
Creation and development of an educational platform utilizing a wiki service

Purpose

To provide teachers and students with a website that would direct them to education material and content.

Target

Secondary schools’ students and teachers.

Output

Online collaborative platform available for learning.

Activity
Technical Assistance

Purpose

To provide technical assistance to technical assistance to the schools.

Target

10 Computer Laboratory Facilities and Learning Hubs

Content

A team of IT technicians will provide technical assistance with the objective of maintaining the computer labs in operational condition.

Output

Maintaining10 computer labs in operating conditions.

Activity
Participation in Technical Training

Purpose

To provide basic IT support training to designated teachers and students allowing them to acquire basic skills in maintaining equipment in operational condition, thereby ensuring the sustainability of the computer labs in the schools.

Target

20 teachers and students / year.

Content

A team of IT trainers will provide basic IT support training to teachers and students.

Output

20 teachers and students trained by year on basic IT support.

Activity
Creation of a Computer Laboratory Maintenance Protocol

Purpose

To develop a Maintenance Protocol to follow on a weekly and monthly basis.

Target

20 teachers and students / year.

Content

We will assist schools in developing a Computer Lab Maintenance Protocol to follow on a weekly and monthly basis.

Output

Lab upkeep, logbooks to monitor computer use.
